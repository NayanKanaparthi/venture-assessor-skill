VALIDATION AND GTM FRAMEWORK

Purpose
Translate research into concrete next steps for learning and initial customer acquisition.

Validation ladder
1. Inquiry
   - ask for reality, not validation
   - understand workflow, pain, current alternatives, and consequences
2. Low-fidelity test
   - mockup, landing page, concept deck, storyboard, manual demo
3. Concierge test
   - simulate the outcome manually
   - prove value before building full product
4. Saleable concierge
   - the product works just enough to solve a real problem
   - the founder manually fills gaps
   - money changes hands if possible
5. Paid pilot / early deployment
   - limited but real implementation
   - focus on value, payment, and feedback

Three early proof conditions
The strongest early validation should show:
1. Value
   The customer gets meaningful benefit.
2. Pays
   The economic buyer pays something or makes a credible commitment.
3. Feedback
   The founder starts a real learning loop with the end user.

PMR design rules
- ask about current behavior before future intent
- ask for examples, not abstract opinions
- prefer “walk me through the last time” over “would you use”
- seek contradiction, not confirmation
- document surprises; they often matter most

First 10 customer logic
A useful GTM hypothesis should answer:
- who exactly should be targeted first
- why they would act now
- how the founder can reach them
- what proof they need
- what the first offer is
- what manual work the founder is willing to do

Three GTM wedge types
1. Founder-led outbound wedge
   - direct outreach
   - narrow ICP
   - high learning speed
2. Channel / partner wedge
   - leverage an existing relationship or workflow owner
3. Content / community wedge
   - useful when pain is public, repeated, and community-shaped

Output requirements
Always produce:
- the top assumptions to test
- one interview plan
- one low-fidelity or concierge test
- one paid test path if plausible
- one recommended first-pass GTM path
