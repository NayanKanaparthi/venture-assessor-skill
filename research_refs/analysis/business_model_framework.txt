BUSINESS MODEL FRAMEWORK

Purpose
Suggest business model options only after the pain, beachhead, and value logic are credible.

Business model principles
- Do not start here.
- Revenue logic follows customer pain and delivery reality.
- A beautiful business model does not rescue a weak problem.

Evaluate candidate models through:
1. Buyer fit
   Who actually pays?
2. Delivery fit
   Can the founder deliver this way at the current stage?
3. Time-to-revenue
   How fast can the founder learn from money?
4. Margin logic
   Is the model viable after manual effort, service load, or acquisition cost?
5. Expansion logic
   Does the model support adjacent growth later?

Common early-stage models
- direct SaaS subscription
- seat-based or usage-based software
- managed-service / hybrid software + service
- paid pilot / implementation fee
- transaction fee / take rate
- marketplace commission
- premium support or onboarding
- channel-led resale / integration revenue

Use with caution
- ad-supported models
- freemium without a credible conversion path
- marketplace models without solving the hard-side acquisition first
- two-sided models without segmenting each side separately

LTV / CoCA reminder
Use LTV / CoCA logic only as a later-stage attractiveness check unless the user explicitly asks.
At the idea-evaluation stage, focus more on:
- ability to get early revenue
- ability to learn quickly
- proof that a customer will pay at all

Output rule
When suggesting business model options:
- give 2 to 4 plausible models
- explain why each fits or does not fit the chosen beachhead
- recommend one primary model and one fallback
