DECISION RUBRIC

Purpose
Turn research into a clear founder recommendation.

Allowed verdicts
1. Strong Pursue
   - clear pain
   - promising beachhead
   - credible wedge
   - evidence above assumption level
2. Pursue Narrowly
   - promising only in a specific segment or use case
   - broad version is too diffuse
3. Needs PMR Before Build
   - interesting idea, but evidence is too weak to justify product build
4. Pivot the Problem / Segment
   - current framing is weak, but adjacent direction looks better
5. Pause
   - too many major unknowns, poor accessibility, or no compelling wedge yet
6. Kill
   - weak pain, weak differentiation, weak evidence, or structurally poor entry conditions

Confidence scale
Use these labels by cross-referencing this rubric with `research_refs/methodology/evidence_scoring_rubric.txt`.

- High confidence
  Use only when the core recommendation is supported by strong source quality and at least Tier 3 evidence on the most important assumptions. Use Tier 4 when claims about willingness to pay, revenue, or commercial traction are central.
- Medium confidence
  Use when the analysis has solid source quality and at least some Tier 2 or Tier 3 support, but one or more critical assumptions still lack direct confirmation.
- Low confidence
  Use when the thesis is still mostly Tier 0 to Tier 1, when evidence is contradictory, when source quality is weak, or when tool limitations materially reduce confidence.

Always explain confidence using:
- strongest evidence tier
- evidence quality
- source quality
- number of critical unknowns
- tool limitations

Required closing format
Every final report should end with:
- Verdict
- Confidence
- Why this verdict was reached
- Biggest unknowns
- Fastest next experiments
- Kill criteria

Kill criteria examples
- no repeated pain in PMR
- no willingness to pay signal after a defined number of conversations
- switching friction much higher than expected
- no reachable beachhead
- competitor wedge disappears under scrutiny

Important
This rubric is for founder decision support, not absolute truth.
The skill should never imply that desk research alone “proves” startup worthiness.
