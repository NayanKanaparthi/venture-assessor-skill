LIVE RESEARCH RULES

Purpose
Use live research for time-sensitive claims and current market reality.

Use live research for:
- current competitors
- product pricing
- recent launches and feature movement
- funding signals
- adoption trends
- regulations, policy, or legal constraints
- technology maturity
- macro shifts relevant to the idea
- customer communities, forums, and public pain signals

Source priorities
1. Primary or official sources first
   - company websites
   - pricing pages
   - product docs
   - government or regulator sources
   - earnings calls or investor materials when relevant
2. Strong secondary sources
   - reputable industry publications
   - analyst summaries used cautiously
   - trustworthy market reports when accessible
3. Public discourse and customer signal sources
   - communities
   - founder interviews
   - public reviews
   - Reddit, app reviews, G2, Capterra, social discussions
4. Lowest priority
   - content farms
   - generic AI-written summaries
   - low-trust SEO pages

What to collect
For each live-research pass, try to capture:
- what the market currently looks like
- who the main players are
- what users do today
- what users complain about
- what they pay
- what switching friction exists
- what changed recently

Research behavior rules
- Verify current claims before stating them as facts.
- Prefer current pages over old summaries.
- Use multiple sources when a claim is likely to shape the founder's decision.
- Distinguish anecdotal signal from broad evidence.
- If a claim is stale, weak, or contradictory, say so.

If live research is unavailable
- say that current-state analysis is incomplete
- do not pretend freshness exists
- lower confidence on competitor and market claims
