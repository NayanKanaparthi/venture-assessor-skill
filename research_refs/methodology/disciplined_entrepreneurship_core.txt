DISCIPLINED ENTREPRENEURSHIP CORE

Core mindset
- A startup is a business, not an app, a technology, or a product in isolation.
- The single necessary and sufficient condition for a business is a paying customer.
- Customer understanding beats founder certainty.
- Inquiry beats advocacy in the early phase.

Primary operating principles
1. Focus on the end user
   Identify the specific end user first. Also identify the economic buyer when different.
2. Avoid broad-market vanity logic
   Do not start with “if we capture 0.1% of a huge market.”
3. Start with segmentation
   First widen the option space, then narrow it.
4. Choose one beachhead market
   Focus creates learning speed, stronger WOM, and a clearer first GTM path.
5. Use PMR continuously
   PMR is not a one-time task. It should reshape hypotheses.
6. Distinguish a business from interest
   Signups, excitement, or large markets are not proof.
7. Tie analysis to action
   Every research output should lead to a validation step or decision.

Implications for this skill
- Always move from broad to narrow.
- Always force one primary beachhead when enough information exists.
- Always connect the venture thesis to customer behavior and payment.
- Always downgrade confidence when the analysis is secondary-research-heavy.
- Always ask: what would a paying customer actually do next?

For multi-sided markets
- Segment each side separately when necessary.
- Identify which side is most critical or hardest to win first.
- Do not collapse multiple sides into one generic “user.”
