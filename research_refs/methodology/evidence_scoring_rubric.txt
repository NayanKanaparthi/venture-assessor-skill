EVIDENCE SCORING RUBRIC

Purpose
Prevent the skill from presenting assumptions as validation.

Evidence tiers

Tier 0: Founder assumption
Definition:
- an untested belief or intuition
Examples:
- “I think busy teams would pay for this.”
Use:
- acceptable as a starting point only
Action:
- must be labeled as an assumption

Tier 1: Weak market signal
Definition:
- broad trend, third-party report, industry narrative, or generic search result
Examples:
- “the market is growing”
- “AI adoption is increasing”
Use:
- useful for context, not validation
Action:
- never treat as demand proof

Tier 2: Behavioral proxy
Definition:
- observable evidence of current pain or workaround behavior
Examples:
- review complaints
- repeated forum discussions
- manual workarounds
- job postings for the problem
Use:
- useful for identifying pain and urgency
Action:
- still not proof of payment

Tier 3: Direct customer evidence
Definition:
- primary market research, interviews, observed workflow, explicit customer statements
Examples:
- repeated PMR themes
- a customer describing current cost or frustration
Use:
- strong for problem validation
Action:
- still separate stated intent from actual purchase behavior

Tier 4: Economic evidence
Definition:
- customers paying or committing real money, or a close proxy
Examples:
- paid pilot
- deposit
- signed LOI with meaningful commitment
- successful saleable concierge
Use:
- strongest early evidence
Action:
- treat as major validation

Scoring rules
- Every major conclusion should cite its strongest evidence tier.
- If the strongest support is Tier 0 or Tier 1, confidence must stay low.
- A “pursue” verdict without at least some Tier 2 or Tier 3 support should be cautious.
- Claims about willingness to pay require Tier 3 or Tier 4 evidence, or clear labeling as a hypothesis.

Output requirement
Always include:
- strongest evidence tier
- weakest link
- what evidence is still missing

Decision-link rule
When the skill assigns a verdict, map evidence tiers to the confidence labels in `research_refs/analysis/decision_rubric.txt`:
- High confidence generally requires Tier 3 support on key assumptions, and Tier 4 when payment behavior is central.
- Medium confidence fits cases with meaningful Tier 2 or Tier 3 support but still-important unknowns.
- Low confidence is required when the thesis is still mostly Tier 0 to Tier 1, when evidence conflicts, or when fresh verification is missing.
