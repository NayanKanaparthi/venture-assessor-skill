BHM TAM RULES

Purpose
Estimate the total addressable market for the chosen beachhead market first.

Primary formula
BHM TAM = number of end customers in the chosen beachhead × average annual revenue per customer to solve the problem

Mandatory rules
- Start with the beachhead only.
- Prefer bottoms-up estimation.
- Show assumptions clearly.
- Use top-down only as a secondary sanity check.
- Distinguish customer count from revenue logic.

Estimation steps
1. Define the beachhead precisely.
2. Estimate the number of end customers in that beachhead.
3. Estimate how much one customer spends in one year to solve the problem.
4. Multiply the two numbers.
5. Sanity-check the result.

Acceptable inputs
- demographic or firmographic counts
- specific user populations
- public pricing pages
- proxy spend on current alternatives
- explicit founder assumptions labeled as such

Red flags
- starting from a giant market and shrinking by an arbitrary capture rate
- mixing global TAM language with a local beachhead thesis
- counting every possible user when only a subset can buy
- using revenue assumptions unrelated to current customer behavior
- forgetting to define annual spend

Confidence guidance
- high confidence: multiple grounded assumptions and market proxies
- medium confidence: one or two assumptions still weak
- low confidence: customer count or annual spend mostly speculative
