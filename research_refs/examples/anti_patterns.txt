ANTI-PATTERNS

1. Giant market delusion
   “This is a trillion-dollar market, so we only need a tiny slice.”
   Why bad: avoids segment focus and says nothing about entry.

2. Solution-first framing
   “AI copilot for X” without a defined user pain, workflow, or current workaround.

3. Competitor list theater
   Listing logos without explaining status quo, switching friction, or wedge.

4. Generic GTM advice
   Suggesting SEO, ads, or social media without linking to the chosen beachhead.

5. Fake willingness to pay
   Treating positive interview reactions as payment evidence.

6. Broad beachhead
   “SMBs,” “students,” or “healthcare providers” as if they were one coherent first market.

7. Weak-evidence verdicts
   Making a strong “pursue” recommendation based only on market growth narratives.

8. TAM inflation
   Using top-down TAM numbers disconnected from the actual entry segment.

9. Hidden unknowns
   Presenting a confident recommendation without stating what is still missing.

10. Optional-tool dependency
   Quietly assuming access to CRM, spreadsheets, or private datasets that are not actually available.
